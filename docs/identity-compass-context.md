# Identity Compass — Product Context Document
## The Origin Insight
This product was born from a personal observation: every time the founder returns to Mexico from the US, a specific weight lifts. The cause isn't simply vacation or rest — it's a return to a felt sense of self. The experience includes:
- A support network oriented around people, not productivity
- Focus on family and friends over work and status
- Distance from the anxiety of US news cycles and immigration uncertainty
- Witnessing people living in genuine fulfillment with what they have
- Real hardship providing perspective that reframes privileged comparison
- A return to feeling calm, confident, in control, supported, and safe
The insight: **Mexico doesn't create a new self. It removes what buries the existing one.**
-----
## The Core Problem
Most people are living as two versions of themselves simultaneously:
**The "Home Self"** — who they are when they feel most grounded, seen, and free. Rooted in memory, relationships, values, and specific moments of clarity. This self knows what matters.
**The "Context Self"** — who they've become in response to external pressure, expectation, and survival. Shaped by environment, culture, professional identity, and ambient noise.
The gap between these two selves creates a specific kind of quiet suffering — hard to name, easy to normalize, rarely addressed directly.
Most people don't realize the gap is closeable without leaving.
-----
## Who This Is For
### Primary Audience: The Intersection of Two Groups
**1. Immigrants and diaspora experiencing cultural dislocation**
People who physically cross between two worlds and feel the shift in identity at the border. They have a clear, embodied experience of what it feels like to return to themselves. They can name the gap. They often lack tools to maintain that groundedness while living abroad.
**2. Anyone whose environment is slowly replacing their identity**
People who haven't crossed a border but have crossed other thresholds — a career change, a new city, a relationship, a class transition. They feel the drift but have no geographic metaphor for it. They just feel vague loss, anxiety, or disconnection from their own values.
### Why This Intersection
The immigrant's experience makes *legible* what everyone is quietly living. Starting with diaspora communities gives the product:
- A specific, passionate, underserved audience who feel immediately seen
- Language and metaphor (home, roots, belonging, crossing back) that resonates universally
- Proof of concept before scaling to the broader human experience of identity drift
*Reference point: Coco was made for Mexican families. It made the entire world cry.*
-----
## The Product's Core Promise
> You were someone before the world told you who to be. This helps you remember — and live as — that person.
This is not a productivity tool. Not a meditation app. Not a journaling platform.
The closest analogues — therapy, spiritual practice, deep friendship — don't scale and aren't on-demand. This product is the **portable, daily, quiet version** of those experiences.
-----
## The Central Metaphor: A Personal Compass
Not a map that tells you where to go. A compass that always shows you where **your** north is.
Every person's north is different. The product doesn't define it. It helps the user:
1. Find it
1. Name it
1. Keep orienting toward it — even when the noise is loud
-----
## The Three Jobs the Product Must Do
### 1. Help people articulate who they are at their most "home"
Not their resume self. Their felt self. Values, textures, relationships, and moments where they felt most alive and most free.
### 2. Make the gap visible
Between who they are and how they are currently living. Not as a diagnosis. Not with judgment. Just as honest, clear observation — a mirror.
### 3. Give them a path to close the gap
Small, specific, personal actions and rituals that don't require flying home. Grounded in *their* articulation of self, not generic wellness advice.
-----
## The MVP: A Single Guided Conversation
The smallest end-to-end slice of this product is **one conversation** that moves a person through all three stages in 10–15 minutes and leaves them with something real at the end.
### Entry Point
A single landing page. One headline:
> *"You were someone before your context made you someone else. Let's find that person."*
No signup. No friction. The value proposition is proven by the experience itself.
-----
### Stage 1 — Surfacing the Home Self
**Question asked:**
*"Describe a specific moment — a memory, a place, a relationship — where you felt most like yourself. Most calm, most grounded, most free."*
The user writes freely. The system listens, then reflects back what it heard — naming the values, textures, and qualities embedded in their response. This is the mirror.
**Goal:** The user sees their Home Self clearly, possibly for the first time in language.
-----
### Stage 2 — Making the Gap Visible
**Question asked:**
*"How is your life right now different from that?"*
Not "what's wrong with your life." Simply — where is the gap? What has been replaced, buried, or quietly surrendered?
**Goal:** The gap becomes named. Real. Not as a problem to fix, but as an honest observation to sit with.
-----
### Stage 3 — One Personal Ritual
Based entirely on what the user shared — generate **one ritual** that is:
- Specific to them (not generic wellness advice)
- Small enough to do tomorrow
- Directly connected to their Home Self as they described it
**Example:** If they described feeling most themselves cooking with their grandmother on Sunday mornings without a phone — the ritual is not "practice mindfulness." It is: *"Cook one thing from memory this week. No recipe. No phone."*
Specificity is what makes it land. It must feel like it could only have been written for this person.
-----
## What the Person Leaves With
After one conversation:
- A clear articulation of who they are at their core
- An honest view of the gap they've been living in
- One concrete thing to do tomorrow that reconnects them to themselves
-----
## Design Principles
**Warm and specific, never sterile and universal**
Generic wellness apps feel like they were made for no one. This product must feel like it was made for *someone* — and that someone happens to be a version of everyone.
**The product doesn't define identity — it excavates it**
The user's Home Self already exists. The product's job is removal, not construction.
**Cultural specificity is celebrated, not flattened**
The metaphors of home, roots, crossing, and belonging are honored — not sanitized into something broadly palatable.
**Small rituals over grand transformations**
The path to closing the gap is daily, small, and contextual. Not a retreat. Not a rebrand.
**The conversation is the product**
At MVP stage, the experience *is* the conversation. No dashboard. No streaks. No gamification. Just honest reflection and one clear next step.
-----
## What This Is Not
- Not a cultural heritage app
- Not a homesickness tool
- Not a productivity or habit tracker
- Not a meditation or breathwork experience
- Not a therapy replacement (though it may complement therapy)
- Not an identity quiz or personality framework
-----
## The Deeper Ambition
The goal is not to help people feel better about their context. The goal is to help people **author their context** rather than be authored by it.
Most people unconsciously become whoever their environment expects them to be. This product is a tool for **identity sovereignty** — the capacity to remain the author of your own self regardless of the pressures and noise of wherever you happen to be living.
-----
*This document is intended as context for AI systems building, extending, or reasoning about this product. The conversation flow, language, and rituals generated should always serve the three core jobs: articulate the Home Self, make the gap visible, and offer one specific path forward.*
