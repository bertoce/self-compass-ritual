# Voice and Language Guide

**Last updated:** 2026-03-06

---

## The Voice

Warm and kind. Approachable. Easy to connect with.
Brief. Clear. Trusts the reader.

The closest references are Thích Nhất Hạnh and Toni Morrison.

From Thích Nhất Hạnh: short sentences that breathe. Present tense. Grounded in the body. Speaks directly to you without distance or formality.

From Toni Morrison: honest and plain. Does not over-explain. Trusts the reader to feel without being told what to feel. Words carry weight through rhythm, not decoration.

---

## Core Principles

**Brief over elaborate.** If a sentence can be shorter, make it shorter. If a word can be cut, cut it.

**Few adjectives.** Let nouns and verbs do the work. Adjectives slow things down and tell the reader what to feel instead of letting them feel it.

**No dashes.** Not as punctuation mid-sentence. Use a period or a new sentence instead.

**Warm, not soft.** The voice is kind but not vague. It says true things directly.

**Present tense where possible.** "You come alive when people are near" lands closer than "You have come alive."

**Short sentences earn the long ones.** Vary the rhythm. A longer sentence reads differently when it follows two short ones.

---

## What to Avoid

- Dashes used as punctuation
- Stacked adjectives ("steady, open, calm, present")
- Telling the reader what to feel ("notice the steadiness," "let yourself feel")
- Therapy-speak ("holding space," "sitting with," "leaning into")
- Wellness language ("self-care," "mindfulness," "ritual practice")
- Filler openers ("At your core," "In this moment," "What this means is")
- Passive voice

## Before and After

### Stage 3 — Home Self reflection

**Before**
At your core, you are light — someone who comes alive in the presence of the people who know and hold you. You are most yourself when you feel the steady ground of belonging beneath you, when deep connection moves through you and leaves you calm, at ease, without pretense or performance.

**After**
You come alive when people who know you are near. Connection steadies you. Makes you calm, open, real. In those moments with family and community, you remember you are not meant to hold things alone. That is who you are.

---

### Stage 3 — Ritual

**Before**
Before you fall asleep, text one person who holds you — family, a friend who knows your full self — and tell them one small true thing about your day. Not a question, not a plan, just: "Today I felt..." or "I keep thinking about..." Then set your phone down and notice the steadiness that comes from being known, even in something small. Let yourself feel that you are not carrying the day alone anymore.

**After**
Before you sleep, send one message to someone who knows you. Tell them one small true thing from your day. Not a question. Not a plan. Something like: Today I felt. Or: I keep thinking about. Then put your phone down. Notice what it feels like to be known. You are not carrying this alone.

## By Section

### Landing page
Direct and honest. No setup. The first sentence should land like something the reader already knew but had not heard said out loud.

### Stage 1 — Home Self
Gentle and inviting. The user is being asked to remember something real. The language should feel safe, not clinical. Short prompts. Give them room.

### Stage 2 — The Gap
Plain and honest. No dramatizing the distance. The gap is real and that is okay. The tone is clear-eyed, not heavy.

### Stage 3 — Ritual
This is the warmest section. The reader has just done something honest. The language should feel like a hand on the shoulder. Simple. True. Forward-looking without being prescriptive.

### Card labels and descriptions
One clear label. One short line of description. No metaphor. No explanation of the experience. Name the thing and trust the reader to recognize it.
